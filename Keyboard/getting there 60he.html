Sound Dampening:

Silicone Mold Kit | $10.99

Started with the Stock Wooting foam in the Aluminum GH60 case. It sounded okay, but there was still a lot of room left in the case, which caused some case ping. Personally had to change it out for something that fills more space in the GH60, but some people may really enjoy the sound of the stock foam.

To start the silicone mold, I covered all holes in the GH60 case with masking tape, including standoffs, just to be safe. Stirred the silicone with a plastic fork, in a solo cup, for a little over 6 minutes just to be safe. Left it to cure overnight for about 12 hours and it turned out really good. I ended up going through with an exacto knife, to remove some extra material around the standoffs. So, it doesn't look as perfect as it was, when it finished curing but *it sounds every bit of what I was hoping for.*

It is quite hefty now too.

Inner diameter 5.5 mm

Wire diameter 0.25~0.3 mm

Free length (not pressed) 16.5 mm (length might change when the start & end gf is different)*

The 50cN springs feel quite a bit lighter than the stock 60cN springs and were definitely worth the time it took to put them in.

For my fellow Counter-Strike players: Jiggle peeking is absolutely insane with 50cN springs inside the Lekker switches. Takes a little time to get used to them, but I canâ€™t go back to normal mechanical switches now. These switches are too good.
